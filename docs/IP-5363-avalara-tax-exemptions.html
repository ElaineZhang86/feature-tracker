IP-5363
Tax Exemption Certificate
Workflow & Design Spec
Audience: Product & Design Feature: AR/AP Tax Exemption Visibility Status: In Spec Last updated: March 2026

Core Design Principle: Cedar Hospital being tax-exempt (AR) does not mean ABC Mechanical is automatically exempt from paying use tax on materials (AP). IP-5363 makes this distinction visible and actionable in the UI — without making wrong assumptions on the user's behalf.

Actors: ABC Mechanical (BuildOps user) · Cedar Hospital (tax-exempt customer) · Steel Supply Co. (vendor, AP) · Avalara (tax engine)

01 Problem

StepWhoActionResult
1 ✅ABC Mechanical → Cedar HospitalAR Invoice createdAvalara applies exempt cert → $0 tax
2 ❌ABC Mechanical → Steel Supply Co.AP Bill createdBuildOps shows $850 use tax — no job context
3 ❌AccountantSees $0 AR + $850 APCalls support — confused
4 ❌AccountantManual tax overrideUndocumented — creates audit risk
AR (Invoice to Hospital)AP (Bill from Vendor)
Who owes tax?ABC Mechanical collects from Cedar HospitalABC Mechanical potentially owes use tax to state
Why exempt?Cedar Hospital has a valid Avalara certABC Mechanical needs their own resale/exemption cert
Auto-linked?✅ Yes❌ No — different legal obligation

02 Solution — New User Flow

StepStatusDescription
1✅ Already worksBuildOps syncs Cedar Hospital to Avalara. Exemption cert applied on AR invoices automatically.
2NewBuildOps pulls cert details back from Avalara and stores locally: Cert ID, Region, Reason, Effective date, Expiry date, Status.
3NewCustomer page shows a green info panel with cert status + guidance that AR and AP tax settings are independent.
4NewThree new editable fields on the Customer record: Use Taxable, AP Work Taxability Type, AR Work Taxability Type — defaults that flow to Jobs.
5NewNew job for Cedar Hospital auto-inherits: isUseTaxable = false, apWorkTaxabilityTypeId = Non-Taxable, arWorkTaxabilityTypeId = Non-Taxable. All overridable per job.
6NewPO shows 🟢 Exempt in Avalara (AR) badge in Tax Details sidebar. Tooltip: informational only — AP taxability controlled separately.
7UpdatedAR Invoice still shows $0 tax (unchanged) + new 🟢 Exempt in Avalara badge + cert summary for transparency.

06 Decision Logic

AR Side — Customer Exemption Status Badge

ConditionBehavior
avalaraCertStatus = Valid✅ Show green info panel on Customer page
avalaraCertStatus = Expired⚠️ Show orange warning on Customer page
avalaraCertStatus = Not found or not syncedHide panel entirely
Cert validShow 🟢 badge on PO / Receipt / Bill — clickable, opens cert summary
Cert validShow 🟢 badge on Job page and AR Invoice — clickable, opens cert summary popup
AlwaysBadge is read-only — never changes tax calculation directly

AP Side — Use Tax & Work Type

  • AP taxability is always configured separately from AR — never inherited from the Avalara cert.
  • When a Job is created, AP fields inherit from Customer.useTaxable and Customer.apWorkTaxabilityTypeId.
  • Job-level overrides always allowed — accountant can change AP settings per job without touching customer defaults.
  • PO lines inherit Job.apWorkTaxabilityTypeId — this drives actual AP tax calculation, not the AR badge.
